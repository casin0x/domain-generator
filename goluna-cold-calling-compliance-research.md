# GoLuna Cold Calling Compliance Research
## AI-Powered Cold Calling for iGaming/Crypto Casino — Regulatory Landscape

**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Scenario:** Company operating from Ethiopia or Hong Kong, no gambling license, using AI voice agents to cold call known iGaming users to convert them to GoLuna (crypto casino platform).

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**This operation carries extreme legal risk.** The combination of (1) no gambling license, (2) AI-generated voice calls, (3) targeting known gamblers with personal data, and (4) promoting an offshore crypto casino creates compounding legal exposure across virtually every target jurisdiction. Multiple countries would classify this as criminal activity, not just a regulatory violation.

**Key risk multipliers:**
- Promoting an **unlicensed** gambling platform is a criminal offense in most regulated markets
- **AI-generated voice calls** are now explicitly regulated (illegal without consent in the US/EU)
- **Cold calling gamblers** using purchased/scraped data violates GDPR and most data protection laws
- Operating from Ethiopia/Hong Kong does **not** shield from enforcement in target countries
- **Crypto casino** adds AML/KYC compliance exposure on top of gambling violations

## 1. ETHIOPIA AS AN OPERATIONS BASE

### Telecom Infrastructure
- **Regulator:** Ethiopian Communications Authority (ECA), established under Proclamation No. 1148/2019
- **Market:** Historically monopoly (Ethio Telecom), Safaricom entered 2022, third license pending (late 2025/early 2026)
- **Call center licensing:** 35,000 birr/year (~$280 USD) license fee under ECA guidelines
- **International rates:** Ethio Telecom hiked international call rates 658% in Oct 2024 due to exchange rate costs; termination rates set at 0.23 birr/min (mobile), gradually decreasing to 0.19 birr by 2029

### Gambling Laws
- **Regulator:** National Lottery Administration (NLA), operating since 1981
- **Legal gambling:** ONLY sports betting and lottery. Online casino, poker, bingo, slots are **explicitly prohibited**
- **Recent crackdown:** Ethiopia revoked ALL sports betting licenses and ordered all betting companies to immediately shut down operations (2025). Deposit channels ordered to stop servicing betting organizations
- **Advertising:** Subject to strict oversight; operators prohibited from advertising in a manner that appeals to children, young people, or suggests gambling solves problems

### Assessment for Outbound Operations
| Factor | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Can you legally operate a call center? | Yes, with ECA license (35,000 birr) |
| Can you make international calls? | Yes, but extremely expensive after 658% rate hike |
| Can you promote gambling from Ethiopia? | **NO** — gambling promotion is regulated and casino gambling is illegal |
| Internet reliability | Poor — Ethiopia has history of internet shutdowns, low broadband penetration |
| **Overall viability** | **LOW.** Legal call center ops possible, but promoting illegal gambling services creates criminal exposure under Ethiopian law. Infrastructure is unreliable and expensive for international outbound. |

## 2. HONG KONG AS AN OPERATIONS BASE

### Telecom & Telemarketing Regulations
- **Regulator:** Office of the Communications Authority (OFCA)
- **DNC Registry:** Exists for fax, SMS, and pre-recorded messages. Person-to-person calls were historically unregulated, but new legislation introduced DNC for live calls
- **Cold calling penalties:** Up to **HK$1 million fine and 5 years imprisonment** for breaching person-to-person telemarketing regulations
- **Data protection:** Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance (PDPO) — Section 35 requires opt-out mechanism; consent required for direct marketing under Part VIA

### Gambling Laws
- **Regulator:** Home and Youth Affairs Bureau
- **Legal gambling:** Hong Kong Jockey Club (HKJC) holds monopoly — horse racing, football betting, Mark Six lottery ONLY
- **No private licensing:** Zero legal pathway for private gambling operators
- **Advertising ban:** Under the Gambling Ordinance (Cap 148), it is **illegal** to advertise offshore bookmaking or betting-related services in Hong Kong
- **Technology platforms** are encouraged to remove gambling ads/websites/apps

### Assessment for Outbound Operations
| Factor | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Can you legally operate a call center? | Yes, with proper business registration |
| Can you make international calls? | Yes, excellent telecom infrastructure |
| Can you promote gambling from HK? | **NO** — advertising offshore gambling is explicitly illegal under the Gambling Ordinance |
| Cold calling restrictions | New laws: up to HK$1M fine + 5 years prison |
| **Overall viability** | **VERY LOW.** Excellent infrastructure but promoting offshore gambling is a criminal offense. Hong Kong actively enforces gambling laws. |

## 4. PROMOTING AN UNLICENSED GAMBLING PLATFORM

### Core Legal Problem

**Promoting a gambling platform without a license is a criminal offense in most regulated markets.** This is not a gray area — it is the single highest-risk element of the proposed operation.

### Criminal vs. Civil vs. Regulatory Penalties

| Jurisdiction Type | Typical Consequence | Example |
|-------------------|---------------------|---------|
| **Strict regulation** (UK, Germany, Netherlands, Italy, France, Spain, Australia) | Criminal prosecution, imprisonment (up to 4-5 years), fines (millions), domain blocking, payment processor blacklisting | UK Gambling Act 2005 s.33: up to 51 weeks imprisonment for advertising unlicensed gambling |
| **Gambling prohibition** (UAE, Thailand, Vietnam) | Criminal prosecution, imprisonment, deportation for foreigners | Thailand: influencers sentenced to 3 months - 3 years for promoting gambling |
| **Emerging regulation** (Brazil, Nigeria, Philippines) | License revocation, regulatory fines, potential criminal charges | Philippines: proposed law to criminalize promoters of illegal gambling sites |
| **Offshore/Curacao** | Curacao itself now enforces: up to 4 years imprisonment for unlicensed gambling operations | Curacao GCA issuing cease-and-desist to unlicensed operators (July 2025) |

### Specific Risks of Unlicensed Promotion

1. **Cross-border enforcement is increasing.** Regulators share intelligence. UKGC, GGL, KSA, ACMA cooperate internationally.
2. **Payment processor blacklisting.** Mastercard/Visa maintain gambling merchant blacklists. Crypto doesn't fully avoid this — fiat on-ramps can be blocked.
3. **Domain seizure.** Multiple jurisdictions can request DNS-level blocks.
4. **Personal criminal liability.** Directors/owners of companies promoting unlicensed gambling can be personally charged. This extends to the individuals making the calls.
5. **RICO/organized crime laws.** In the US, illegal gambling promotion can trigger federal RICO charges (up to 20 years). The Travel Act (18 USC 1952) prohibits facilitating unlawful gambling across state/international lines.
6. **Money laundering charges.** Processing deposits for an unlicensed gambling operation can be charged as money laundering, which carries severe penalties globally.

## 5. AI VOICE AGENT — ADDITIONAL REGULATORY LAYER

### The AI Dimension Makes Everything Worse

Using AI-generated voices for cold calling adds a **separate layer of illegality** in many jurisdictions:

| Jurisdiction | AI Voice Call Status |
|--------------|---------------------|
| **USA (FCC)** | AI-generated voices in robocalls declared **illegal** (Feb 2024). TCPA applies. Prior express written consent required. Proposed rules require in-call disclosure of AI use. |
| **EU** | AI Act (2024) classifies AI systems interacting with persons as requiring transparency. Must disclose AI use. GDPR applies to all data processing. |
| **UK** | ICO guidance requires transparency about automated decision-making. PECR applies. |
| **India** | TRAI prohibits calls from mobile numbers for telemarketing. AI voice agent calling would need to use 140-series numbers. |
| **Australia** | ACMA Do Not Call Register applies. Automated calls require explicit consent. |

**Key point:** Even in jurisdictions where human cold calling might be technically possible with compliance, AI-generated voice calls face additional or total prohibition.

## 7. LEAD SOURCING — WHERE TO GET iGAMING USER LISTS

### Available Methods and Their Legality

| Source | Description | Legal Status | Risk |
|--------|-------------|--------------|------|
| **Data brokers (gambling-specific)** | Companies selling lists of known gamblers by country, spend level, game preference | **Illegal in EU/UK** under GDPR without consent. Questionable everywhere. | **EXTREME** — buying gambling user data without consent violates GDPR Art. 6/9 (special category data), LGPD, POPIA, etc. Gambling behavior is sensitive personal data in most frameworks. |
| **Affiliate data sharing** | Affiliates sharing their registered user databases | **Illegal** without explicit user consent for third-party marketing. Violates affiliate ToS. | **HIGH** — affiliates sharing data to third parties without consent = GDPR violation for both parties |
| **Scraping gambling sites** | Extracting user data from forums, social media, betting communities | **Illegal** under CFAA (US), Computer Misuse Act (UK), GDPR. Violates ToS. | **EXTREME** — criminal offense in multiple jurisdictions |
| **Social media targeting** | Building lists from public gambling group members (Facebook, Telegram, Reddit) | **Gray area** — public data but using it for direct marketing requires legal basis | **HIGH** — GDPR legitimate interest unlikely to apply for gambling promotion |
| **Previous customer data** | Using data from other gambling platforms you've operated | Legal only with proper consent and privacy notices | **MEDIUM** if properly consented, **HIGH** if repurposed without consent |
| **Survey/competition sign-ups** | Collecting numbers via "win free bets" campaigns with marketing consent | **Legal** if consent is genuine, specific, informed, and freely given | **LOW-MEDIUM** — this is the only legally defensible approach |
| **Telecom data (carrier partnerships)** | Carriers selling subscriber data with behavioral signals | **Illegal in most jurisdictions** without subscriber consent | **EXTREME** — carrier would face massive fines |

### GDPR Special Category Data Issue

**Critical:** Under GDPR Article 9, data revealing gambling behavior could be classified as data concerning health (gambling addiction is a recognized medical condition) or at minimum constitutes profiling under Article 22. Processing this data requires **explicit consent** — not just legitimate interest.

**This means:** You cannot buy a list of "known iGaming users" and cold call them in any GDPR jurisdiction without their explicit prior consent to receive gambling marketing calls. Period.

### Practical Reality

The only legally defensible lead sourcing approach is:
1. Run your own opt-in campaigns (social media ads, landing pages, competitions)
2. Collect phone numbers with explicit consent for gambling marketing calls
3. Then call those opted-in leads

This defeats the purpose of "cold calling" — it becomes warm outbound to consented leads, which is just normal marketing.

### What Could Actually Happen

1. **Regulatory fines** — GDPR fines up to 4% of global turnover or EUR 20M. UKGC, KSA, GGL fines in millions.

## 9. RECOMMENDATIONS

### What NOT To Do
- Do not cold call any person who has not explicitly opted in to receive gambling marketing calls
- Do not use AI-generated voices without disclosure and consent
- Do not purchase or scrape lists of iGaming users
- Do not promote an unlicensed platform in any regulated market
- Do not operate from Ethiopia or Hong Kong for gambling promotion (illegal in both)

### If You Still Want Outbound Voice as a Channel
1. **Get licensed.** Curacao (new regime), or target-market specific licenses. Without a license, every other compliance step is moot.
2. **Build opt-in lists.** Run digital campaigns to collect phone numbers with explicit, granular consent for gambling voice marketing.
3. **Use consented warm outbound** — call people who registered but didn't deposit. This is legal, effective, and industry-standard.
4. **Disclose AI use.** If using AI voice agents, disclose it at the start of every call.
5. **Respect DNC lists.** Scrub against every applicable registry before calling.
6. **Target Tier 3 markets** (Kenya, Nigeria, Mexico, Argentina) where enforcement is lighter — but still get licensed and build opt-in lists.

### Better Acquisition Channels for GoLuna
- **Telegram/Discord community building** — crypto-native audiences, low CAC ($20-80)
- **Influencer/streamer partnerships** — Twitch, Kick, YouTube. Doubles intent among under-30s.
- **Affiliate marketing** — 50%+ of FTD volume globally comes through affiliates
- **SEO/content** — Lowest CAC ($30-100), compounds over time
- **Referral programs** — Existing players bring friends, lowest effective CAC
