Section 01

What is FTG?

The Core Technology

FTG is a separate company (Rapidfire Unicorn Ltd., London) with a patented real-time transaction engine that sits between games and blockchain. FTG handles only the money — game studios handle the gameplay. GoLuna partners with FTG to integrate their infrastructure into our platform.

Core Mechanic

When Player A hits Player B, money moves instantly from B to A. Not at match end. The moment the server reports the action.

Key differentiator: FTG is infrastructure, not a game. Any studio can build on it — game-agnostic financial transactions triggered by gameplay events. GoLuna leverages this as a collaboration partner, not as the technology owner.

Why P2E Failed (and Why FTG Won't)

P2E Death Spiral

  • Revenue depended on new player inflow (Ponzi dynamics)
  • Axie: ATH to −97% in 18 months
  • StepN, SAND, Decentraland — same model, same collapse
  • Academic consensus: unsustainable economies tied to token supply expansion

FTG Sustainable Model

  • Zero-sum like poker — money from other players
  • Stablecoins (USDT/USDC), no token volatility
  • Works without new entrants — existing players sustain economy
  • Revenue = rake on transactions (proven model for 200+ years)

Section 07

Risks & Mitigation (Honest)

Risk HIGH

Shark tank problem — skilled players drain newcomers, causing rapid churn.

#1 reason PVP games fail historically
Mitigation

ELO/MMR matchmaking, beginner-protected lobbies, max loss caps per session.

Risk MEDIUM

Regulatory reclassification — skill games ruled as gambling in some jurisdictions.

Active in US primarily, not GoLuna's target GEOs
Mitigation

Target GEOs (Brazil, Nigeria, UAE) don't classify skill games as gambling. Curacao license covers it.

Risk HIGH

Per-action model unproven at scale — no platform has hit mass adoption.

No platform has proven this model at 10K+ users
Mitigation

Start with 1–2 games. Casino + sports remain revenue base. Test before scaling.

Risk MEDIUM

Losers churn — 50% of players lose every game by definition.

Solvable with matchmaking and low stakes
Mitigation

$1 buy-in playgrounds, game variety, social features, tournament guaranteed pools.

Risk LOW

No public traction data for Hotshot — no published user metrics.

GoLuna's value isn't dependent on Hotshot's success
Mitigation

FTG provides infrastructure; GoLuna controls game selection, marketing, and player experience on its own platform.

Retargeting & Cross-Sell

Every FTG sign-up gives GoLuna three retargeting channels to drive engagement across the platform:

  • Email — Welcome sequences, match results, deposit bonuses for casino/sports. Personalized based on game preferences.
  • Wallet / Push — On-chain activity triggers: “Your balance is idle — try these slots” or “Live match odds on tonight's game.”
  • SMS — High-value reactivation for churned players. Tournament invites, time-limited bonuses. Especially effective in Brazil, Nigeria, Kenya.
  • Telegram Bot — Near-100% deliverability (vs. email's 10–30% open rate). Balance alerts, tournament reminders, personalized promos. The most underutilized retention channel in crypto gaming.

Why It Works

  • PVP sidebar shows live casino & sports odds — passive exposure every session
  • Shared wallet: PVP winnings are immediately playable on casino/sports, zero friction
  • Gamers who wager on skill games have already crossed the psychological barrier to real-money play
  • DraftKings proved this model: cross-sell cut their CAC by 40% YoY, now launching a “Super App” combining all verticals

Acquisition Edge

  • “Play for real money” PVP ads reach audiences that ignore casino ads
  • Gamers on Twitch, YouTube, Discord — untapped by traditional gambling marketing. Germany's GlüNeuRStV exempts skill games from casino regulation.
  • Lower CAC: PVP clips and tournaments are inherently shareable. Slots aren't. Brazil + Argentina = cheapest GEOs (0.4–0.7x baseline CAC).
  • Organic virality: “I just won $50 in a real-money PVP match” — posts itself
