Section 03

Why P2E Failed (and Why FTG Won't)

P2E Death Spiral

  • Money came from new players buying tokens (Ponzi-like inflow dependency)
  • Axie Infinity: ATH to −99% in 18 months
  • StepN, SAND, Decentraland all collapsed on same model
  • Cornell study: "unsustainable game economy tied to ever-expanding token supply"
  • When new players stopped coming, the economy collapsed instantly

FTG Sustainable Model

  • Money comes from other players (zero-sum like poker)
  • Uses stablecoins (USDT/USDC) — no token volatility
  • Existing players can play indefinitely without new entrants
  • Revenue = rake on transactions (like poker, proven for 200+ years)
  • No token dependency, no inflation risk, no treasury drain

Section 07

Risks & Mitigation (Honest)

Risk

Shark tank problem — skilled players drain newcomers. High-skill players dominate low-skill, causing rapid churn of new users.

Mitigation

Skill-based matchmaking (ELO/MMR), beginner playgrounds with protected lobbies, balance protection caps limiting max loss per session.

Risk

Regulatory reclassification — skill games ruled as gambling in certain jurisdictions.

Mitigation

Target GEOs (Brazil, Nigeria, UAE) don't classify skill games as gambling. Curacao license covers skill-based gaming. Avoid US states with restrictive rulings.

Risk

Per-action model unproven at scale — no platform has achieved mass adoption with per-action micro-transactions.

Mitigation

Start with 1–2 games as experimental vertical. Casino + sports remain revenue base. Test with real users before scaling investment.

Risk

Losers churn — PVP retention is inherently harder than PvE. 50% of players lose every game by definition.

Mitigation

Low-stakes playgrounds ($1 buy-in), multiple game types for variety, social features, tournament formats with guaranteed prize pools.

Risk

No public traction data for Hotshot — FTG's flagship demo game has no published user metrics.

Mitigation

FTG is infrastructure, not a single game. GoLuna hosts games on FTG — we control game quality, marketing, and player experience independently.
