Strategy 01

AI Cold Calling for iGaming — Compliance Scan

The Concept

Leverage AI voice agent technology (developed by a partner studio) to cold call known iGaming users, converting them to GoLuna. Operations from Ethiopia or Hong Kong, no gambling license initially. Calls target leads who are existing iGaming players.

Verdict: Not Viable As Described

The combination of no gambling license + AI voice agents + cold calling gamblers with purchased data + promoting a crypto casino creates compounding criminal exposure in virtually every target market. This is not a gray area — multiple countries classify this as criminal activity.

Operations Base

Ethiopia vs Hong Kong Assessment

Ethiopia

  • Call center license: 35,000 birr (~$280/year) — legal
  • International call rates spiked 658% in Oct 2024
  • Gambling promotion is illegal — Ethiopia revoked ALL sports betting licenses in 2025
  • Poor internet infrastructure, history of shutdowns

Hong Kong

  • Excellent telecom infrastructure
  • Advertising offshore gambling is criminal under Gambling Ordinance (Cap 148)
  • Gambling promotion penalties: up to HK$5M fine + 7 years prison
  • HKJC monopoly — zero private gambling licensing

Neither Location Provides Legal Cover

Ethiopia bans gambling promotion entirely. Hong Kong criminalizes offshore gambling advertising. Both would expose operators to prosecution before any target-country laws even apply.

Three Compounding Risk Layers

  • No license — Promoting unlicensed gambling is criminal in most regulated markets (UK: up to 51 weeks prison for providing facilities, 6 months for advertising; Curacao: criminal charges under LOK; Thailand: up to 1 year)
  • AI voice calls — FCC declared AI robocalls illegal without consent (Feb 2024). EU AI Act requires transparency disclosure. Separate violation layer.
  • Gambling user data — Under GDPR, gambling behavior data may constitute special category data (health-related, per multiple DPA interpretations). Buying/scraping lists without explicit consent is illegal in EU/UK/Brazil/SA.

Cold Calling Math (Optimistic Scenario)

  • 10,000 calls × 20% connect rate = 2,000 answered
  • 5% interested = 100 click-throughs
  • 30% register = 30 sign-ups

GDPR Article 9: Gambling behavior data may constitute special category data where it reveals health information (gambling addiction is a recognized medical condition). Multiple DPAs interpret this strictly. Processing requires explicit consent, not just legitimate interest. You cannot buy a list of "known iGaming users" and cold call them in any GDPR jurisdiction. The only legal approach (opt-in) defeats the purpose of cold calling.

Recommendation

Pivoted Approach — What To Do Instead

If You Still Want Outbound Voice

  • Get licensed first — Curacao new regime ($150K+), or target-market licenses. Without a license, all other compliance is moot.
  • Build opt-in lists — Digital campaigns to collect phone numbers with explicit, granular consent for gambling marketing calls.
  • Warm outbound only — Call people who registered but didn't deposit. Legal, effective, and industry-standard.
  • Disclose AI use — EU AI Act + FCC require disclosure. Say it at the start of every call.
  • Target Tier 3 first — Kenya, Nigeria, Mexico, Argentina — lighter enforcement, but still get licensed and use opt-in lists.
