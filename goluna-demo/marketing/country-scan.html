Methodology

How the Composite Score Works

Each country is scored 1–10 across 10 dimensions. The composite score is the sum (max 100). Higher = better for GoLuna. Scores are based on Chainalysis 2025, ICLG gambling law reports 2025–2026, Statista, and verified news sources.

Mkt — Market Size

Total gambling revenue, online share, growth rate. Bigger market = higher score.

Crypto — Crypto Adoption

% population using crypto, USDT penetration. Based on Chainalysis 2025 index.

Reg — Regulatory Safety

Is offshore gambling tolerated? Active enforcement? Higher = safer for operator.

Comp — Low Competition

Market saturation. Is Stake.com dominant? Higher = more room for new entrants.

Digi — Digital Access

Internet penetration, smartphone usage, mobile-first culture.

Pay — Payment Infra

Can players easily buy crypto? Fiat on-ramps available? P2P trading active?

ARPU — Revenue Potential

GDP per capita proxy. Disposable income for gambling. Higher = more $ per player.

Lang — Localization Ease

English proficiency? Localization cost. 10 = English-native, 3 = full translation needed.

Mktg — Marketing Feasibility

Can you run ads? Influencer ecosystem? Active Telegram/Discord communities?

Legal — Low Legal Risk

Prosecution risk for operators. Historical enforcement. Higher = less risk.

Top 10 Deep Dive

Market Notes — Top 10 Countries

1. Nigeria — 73/100 T1

#6 Chainalysis 2025 (down from #2 in 2023 after CBN crackdowns). ~42% crypto engagement. USDT P2P trading dominates — Nigeria leads Africa in stablecoin volume. Gambling regulation fragmented across states, crypto casinos not specifically prohibited. English-speaking, median age 18, massive Telegram communities.

Risk: Low ARPU ($2,100 GDP). Volume-dependent monetization.

2. New Zealand — 70/100 T1

Crypto casinos explicitly fall outside the Gambling Act 2003. The Online Casino Gambling Bill (introduced June 2025) has not passed yet — giving a 2+ year window. $48K GDP per capita, English-native, low competition from crypto operators, 95%+ internet penetration.

Risk: Small population (~5.2M). New bill will eventually close the gap.

3. Brazil — 69/100 T2

22.1M active online bettors, ~$7B market, 20.6% crypto ownership. 23.4% of global Bitcoin casino traffic. But SPA has blocked 19,180+ illegal URLs and closed 483 bank accounts since Jan 2025. Massive market rapidly moving from grey to black for unlicensed operators.

Risk: Active enforcement accelerating. Payment blocking via Central Bank. Could move to “Avoid” within 12 months.

4. Kenya — 69/100 T2

VASP Act 2025 legalized crypto (commenced Nov 4, 2025). Gambling widely popular and culturally accepted. Crypto casinos in grey area — regulation doesn't address crypto payments. English-speaking, strong M-Pesa mobile money culture, active P2P crypto trading.

Risk: Low GDP (~$2,100). Crypto on-ramp friction via M-Pesa.

5. Philippines — 68/100 T2

Post-POGO ban (Dec 2024) collapsed offshore gambling infrastructure, but domestic demand is massive. Top 10 Chainalysis, strong English proficiency, deep gambling culture. GCash-to-USDT corridors well established.

Risk: PAGCOR could extend enforcement to crypto platforms. Low ARPU (~$3,900 GDP).

6. Ghana — 67/100 T2

VASP Bill signed into law Dec 30, 2025 — crypto now legal. 3M Ghanaians (~17% of adults) already use crypto. English-speaking, low competition, gambling regulation doesn't target crypto casinos. Natural expansion from Nigeria.

Risk: Very low GDP (~$2,400). Internet penetration ~68%. Small total market.

7. Argentina — 66/100 T2

Chronic inflation drives massive crypto adoption (#20 Chainalysis, ~8% of population). USDT used as savings vehicle by millions. Gambling culturally embedded, provincial regulation. Offshore crypto casinos face minimal enforcement. VPN usage high.

Risk: Fragmented provincial regulation. Peso volatility complicates fiat economics. Spanish localization required.

8. South Africa — 66/100 T2

Most developed gambling market in Africa (~$3B annual revenue). 19.6% crypto ownership (top 5 globally). English-speaking, $6,500 GDP — highest ARPU in Africa. FSCA regulates crypto under FATF standards but enforcement against offshore gambling is limited.

Risk: National Gambling Board relatively active by African standards. Remote Gambling Bill discussed but not enacted.

9. Vietnam — 64/100 T2

#4 Chainalysis 2025. Gambling illegal for citizens but enforcement against players using offshore platforms is practically non-existent — millions play unregulated. USDT dominant in P2P markets. Police target domestic operators, not offshore websites.

Risk: All gambling technically illegal. Periodic busts create headlines. Vietnamese localization required (low English). Payment disruptions when authorities target corridors.

10. Hong Kong — 64/100 T2

$52K GDP per capita, 233% YoY growth in bank digital asset transactions (H1 2025). Stablecoin Ordinance (Aug 2025) legitimizes crypto. Tech-savvy population, English widely spoken. Enforcement against offshore platforms limited in practice despite Jockey Club monopoly.

Risk: Small population (~7.5M). Gambling Ordinance technically prohibits all unauthorized gambling. China influence could trigger crackdown.
