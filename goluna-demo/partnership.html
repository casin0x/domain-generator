01 Plan Summary

In a single paragraph: when GoLuna’s monthly Net Profit exceeds $25,000, ten percent of that month’s Net Profit is allocated to the operating-team loyalty envelope. Half of the envelope (5% of NP) is paid out monthly to the three current members — 2% to Owl, 1.5% each to Toro and Gibbiana. The other half (5% of NP) accumulates in a Reserve Pool earmarked for future loyalty-plan deployment: new hires joining the plan, scope-expansion bumps for existing members, or discretionary milestone bonuses. Each individual paid share is capped at $25,000 per month. Settlement is monthly, based on that month’s realised Net Profit. The plan applies exclusively to the named members and the Reserve; investor distributions continue to be governed separately by the company’s equity arrangements.

Three guiding principles.  (1) The plan only pays when the business is genuinely profitable. (2) Both sides — the team and the investors — benefit from the same outcome: sustained monthly Net Profit. (3) The figures, mechanics, and review triggers are written down in advance so neither side has to negotiate them in the moment.

03 How the Plan Works

The mechanics are deliberately simple. There is one rate, one threshold, one cap, and one distribution method. Each operates independently of the others, which keeps the monthly settlement straightforward to calculate and audit.

Mechanic Definition
Activation threshold The plan pays out in any month where GoLuna’s Net Profit (also referred to as EBITDA) is $25,000 or higher. Below this figure, the pool contributes nothing for that month and base salaries continue unchanged.
Pool envelope 10% of that month’s Net Profit, calculated on the full Net Profit figure (not the amount above the threshold). For example, at $100,000 monthly Net Profit, the envelope is $10,000.
Envelope split The envelope is divided in half: 5% of NP is paid out monthly to the current operating team, and 5% of NP accumulates in the Reserve Pool for future loyalty-plan deployment.
Allocation (paid half) Of the 5% paid out, the allocation is 2% of NP to Owl and 1.5% of NP to each of Toro and Gibbiana. Owl’s slightly higher share reflects his cross-cutting scope (finance, investor reporting, and operations PM).
Reserve Pool The 5% reserve accumulates as an earmarked loyalty obligation in the company’s books. The underlying cash remains in the operating account until deployed. Reserve deployment requires mutual consent of founder and cap-table holders, and may fund: (a) new hires joining the loyalty plan, (b) scope-expansion bumps for existing members, (c) discretionary milestone bonuses to existing members. Reserve accrual pauses when the earmarked balance reaches a soft cap of $500,000; the figure can be adjusted by mutual consent.
Per-person cap Each member’s monthly paid share is capped at $25,000. Because the rates differ, the cap binds at different Net Profit levels: Owl’s cap binds at $1.25M monthly NP (2% × $1.25M = $25K), Toro’s and Gibbiana’s caps each bind at $1.67M monthly NP (1.5% × $1.67M = $25K). Once a paid share is at the cap, the corresponding excess does not add to the Reserve; it stays with the company.
Settlement Settled monthly, in arrears, based on that month’s realised Net Profit. There is no annual reconciliation, no clawback on lower months, and no carryforward of unpaid amounts.
Base correction A separate threshold at $50,000 monthly Net Profit: when first reached, Owl’s base salary moves permanently from $1,000 to $2,000 per month. The pool itself begins paying at $25K NP (above); the base correction is held back until $50K NP. This is a one-time event funded by the company, not from the pool.

05 Reasoning — Why This Structure

The plan was designed to be defensible from both sides of the table. The two cards below set out the reasoning specifically from each audience’s perspective. The same structure underlies both views; the framing is different because the audiences are different.

For Investors

Why this plan protects capital and aligns incentives

  • No cost during the build phase. The pool activates only above $25K monthly Net Profit, so it contributes nothing to burn during the runway-spend period when capital efficiency matters most.
  • Paid only on real profit. The pool is calculated on Net Profit, not GGR — preventing any scenario where the team is rewarded for top-line growth that loses money on bonuses, provider fees, or marketing.
  • Half the envelope stays in the company. Five percent of NP is paid out monthly; the other five percent accumulates in the Reserve Pool. The Reserve is an earmarked future-loyalty obligation, not restricted cash — the company retains the use of those funds in the operating account until deployment.
  • Effective paid rate tightens at scale. Up to $1.25M monthly NP, paid comp sits at 5% of NP. Above that, individual caps begin to bind and the paid rate declines: 4.5% at $1.67M NP, 3.75% at $2M NP, 1.5% at $5M NP. The 5% Reserve accrual also pauses once the soft cap is reached.
  • Bounded total exposure. Combined annual paid cost, at full per-person cap, is $900K. Reserve accrual is capped at a $500K soft balance. Total maximum committed envelope per year sits in the $900K–$1.4M range before any Reserve deployment, and any Reserve deployment is itself bounded by mutual consent.
  • Investor distributions are unaffected. Equity-based distributions remain governed by the cap table and shareholders’ agreement. The pool is an operating-expense line above the distribution waterfall, applying equally to all equity holders.
For the Operating Team

Why this plan recognises your contribution

  • Your share grows with the company’s success. Each share scales linearly with Net Profit through the entire band where most of the work happens, with no ceiling until the individual cap is reached.
  • Predictable and transparent. Each member’s share is a fixed percentage of last month’s Net Profit (2% Owl, 1.5% Toro, 1.5% Gibbiana). No performance review, no political weighting, no behind-the-scenes decision.
  • Differentiated shares reflect scope. Owl’s 2% acknowledges his cross-cutting scope — finance, investor reporting, and operations PM. Toro and Gibbiana’s 1.5% each reflects their more focused functional roles. The differential is small enough not to create friction and aligned with the responsibilities each person carries.
  • Owl’s base correction is built in. Pool share applies to all three from $25K NP; Owl’s base then moves to parity at the $50K NP mark, funded by the company — not from the pool.
  • Settled monthly, no clawback. Each month stands on its own. Strong months are paid in full at month-end. No annual reconciliation, no clawback on softer months, no carryforward to argue over.
  • Re-evaluation is rule-based. The plan is reviewed only when the specific written triggers in §07 occur. Between those triggers, the structure stays as written.

07 Operating Rules

The rules below cover the situations not addressed by the basic mechanics: changes to the team, performance issues, departures, and review triggers. They are written here, in advance, so neither side needs to negotiate them under pressure if a situation later arises.

Rule Detail
Fixed allocation The plan is permanently allocated to the three named members: Toro, Owl, and Gibbiana. Future hires, advisors, and contractors receive compensation under separate arrangements appropriate to their roles, and do not receive any share of this pool.
Departure (default rule) If a member departs the company, their pool share ends on the departure date. There is no clawback on shares paid in prior months, and the departing member’s share is not redistributed to the remaining members. The company retains the departing member’s share from that month onward.
Departure (alternative) If the parties prefer, a sixty-day tail can be agreed, under which the departing member continues to receive their share for the two months following their last day. This option is not the default; it applies only if explicitly agreed in writing.
Investor compensation Investors are compensated through equity returns (dividends, capital gains on exit), as governed by the cap table and shareholders’ agreement. They do not draw from this pool, and the pool does not change the proportion of equity-distributable profit they receive relative to one another.
Performance and conduct Pool share is tied to active membership in the role, not to individual key-performance indicators. If a serious performance or conduct issue arises, the conversation is a role-change or departure conversation — not a reduction of the member’s share. This keeps the plan administratively simple and free of political pressure.
Absences and time off Ordinary holiday, sickness, and personal leave do not affect pool entitlement. The share follows continued membership in the role, not hours worked in a given month.
Review triggers The plan is reviewed by mutual consent in any of three situations: (a) total operational headcount exceeds six; (b) any member’s role materially expands beyond its current scope; or (c) the per-person cap binds for three consecutive months. Between these triggers, the structure remains as written and is not subject to renegotiation.
Documentation Once the first qualifying month occurs, the plan is reduced to a side letter between the company and each member, signed by both parties and a representative of the cap-table holders. This dashboard page is the working reference, not the agreement of record.
Tax handling Payments under this plan are processed through the member’s standard compensation channel and are taxed as employment or contractor income in the relevant jurisdiction. The company is responsible for any applicable withholding; the member is responsible for personal tax filings in their country of residence.
08 Scope of This Plan

For clarity, this section sets out explicitly what the plan does and does not cover. It is intended to prevent any future confusion about whether the arrangement extends to a particular situation, person, or financial instrument.

This plan covers

What is in scope

  • Three named members: Toro, Owl, and Gibbiana, in their current operational roles.
  • Monthly paid compensation (5% of NP split as 2% Owl, 1.5% Toro, 1.5% Gibbiana) tied to monthly Net Profit above the activation threshold.
  • The Reserve Pool (5% of NP accrual) and its deployment rules, including the mutual-consent requirement and $500K soft cap.
  • The base correction for Owl at the $50K NP threshold.
  • The operating rules set out in §07, including departure, performance, review triggers, and documentation.
This plan does not cover

What is out of scope

  • Equity, share options, or any ownership interest in GoLuna. These remain governed exclusively by the cap table and shareholders’ agreement.
  • Future hires, contractors, or advisors, who will receive separate compensation appropriate to their roles.
  • Investor distributions, which continue to be paid according to existing equity arrangements and are not altered by this plan.
  • Founder or executive compensation for any individual not named in §02. Such arrangements, if any, are documented separately.
  • One-time bonuses, sign-on payments, or discretionary awards, none of which form part of this plan.
09 Questions Frequently Raised

The questions below have been gathered from both sides of the table during the design of this plan. The tag next to each question indicates the audience from whom it most often comes, but the answer is the same for everyone.

Investor Why a ten percent envelope, with five percent paid and five percent held in Reserve?
Ten percent is the standard pool size for profit-share arrangements at early operating teams in technology and digital businesses. Paying only half of it (5% of NP) to the current operating team keeps current-cash burn conservative, while the other half (5% of NP) accumulating in the Reserve Pool gives the company a pre-funded mechanism to bring on new hires under the loyalty plan, to bump existing members’ shares as their scope expands, or to pay discretionary milestone bonuses — without needing to re-open the envelope conversation with investors each time. The full 10% is the committed envelope; how the second 5% is deployed is governed by mutual consent.
Both How does the Reserve Pool actually work in practice?
The Reserve is an accounting earmark, not restricted cash. Each profitable month, an entry equal to 5% of that month’s Net Profit is added to a Reserve balance tracked in the company’s books. The underlying cash stays in the operating account and remains available for working-capital use until the Reserve is actually deployed. Deployment requires mutual consent of founder and cap-table holders and may fund: (a) a new hire joining the loyalty plan, (b) a scope-expansion bump to an existing member’s paid share, or (c) a discretionary milestone bonus. Reserve accrual pauses once the earmarked balance reaches a soft cap of $500,000 to prevent indefinite hoarding.
Team Why is some of the share held in Reserve rather than paid to us now?
The shares paid to you now (2% Owl, 1.5% Toro, 1.5% Gibbiana) are yours unconditionally each month the company is profitable. The Reserve sits alongside them as a pre-committed pool that the company can use to expand the loyalty plan over time — whether that means bringing on a fourth member, increasing one of your shares as your scope grows, or paying a one-off bonus on a major milestone. If the company never grows the team and never bumps your scope, the Reserve simply accumulates; it can also be partially released back to general company use by mutual consent under the review triggers in §07.
Investor What stops the paid portion from growing without limit?
Two structural limits on paid comp. First, each member’s monthly paid share is capped at $25,000, placing an absolute ceiling on individual compensation. Second, the paid allocation is fixed to three named individuals; new hires do not automatically enter the paid plan, regardless of seniority or role. The maximum paid annual exposure is $900,000, reached only when the business is generating ~$20M in annual Net Profit. Reserve accrual is separately bounded by the $500K soft cap.
Investor Does this affect my equity returns?
No. The pool is an operating-expense line above the distribution waterfall, the same way salaries are. The proportion of distributable profit each shareholder receives is unchanged. Total distributable profit is reduced by the pool’s size each month, but that reduction is the cost of having an operating team that delivered the profit in the first place.
Team Is this in addition to my base salary, or instead of it?
In addition. Your monthly base salary continues to be paid in full, unchanged. The pool share is added on top whenever the activation threshold is reached. For Owl specifically, the base salary changes at the first qualifying month (the one-time correction described in §02 and §03), and the pool share is added on top of the new base.
Team What if Net Profit fluctuates from month to month?
Each month is settled on its own. In a strong month, you receive the share for that month at the end of that month. In a softer month, you receive a smaller share or no share. There is no annual averaging, no clawback on lower months, and no carryforward of unpaid amounts. The pool is straightforwardly the result of last month’s actual figures.
Team What if I leave the company?
The default rule, set out in §07, is that your share ends on your departure date and there is no clawback on amounts already paid. Your share is not redistributed to the other two members; the company simply retains it from that point on. If the parties prefer, a sixty-day tail can be agreed in writing, but this is not automatic.
Both Will the plan change later?
Only by mutual consent, and only when one of the three review triggers in §07 occurs. The triggers are written in advance precisely so that neither side has to argue for a review based on a subjective judgement; the trigger either applies or it does not. Between triggers, the plan remains as written.
Team Why is Owl’s share higher than Toro’s and Gibbiana’s?
Owl’s role spans finance, investor reporting, and operations PM — functions that touch every other part of the business and require coordination work that the other two roles don’t. The 0.5% differential is the smallest meaningful step we could give while acknowledging that scope. Toro’s and Gibbiana’s 1.5% reflects their focused functional roles, which are equally essential but narrower in scope. If the scope of any role changes materially, the percentages can be revisited under the review triggers in §07.
Investor Why is the activation threshold set at $25,000 monthly Net Profit?
Below this level the company is either at break-even or only modestly profitable, and the absolute pool amount would be small ($2,500 at the threshold itself, of which each member receives roughly $833). The threshold ensures that the plan only contributes to operational cost once the business has comfortably passed break-even, while still being low enough that the team sees movement as soon as profitability becomes meaningful.
Both Why does Owl receive a base correction, and why is it held back until $50K NP rather than $25K NP?
Owl’s base was set lower than Toro’s and Gibbiana’s at inception for practical reasons that no longer apply once the company is profitable. The correction is held back to the $50K NP threshold (rather than the $25K pool activation) so that the additional company-funded cost stays out of the burn line until profitability is clearly established. From $25K NP, Owl receives the same pool share as the others; at $50K NP, his base moves to parity, and from that point on the three are treated identically. The correction is company-funded so it does not reduce the shared pool.
Both Where do the “GGR required” figures come from, and what costs are included?
From the variable-cost stack and M7+ fixed-cost run-rate in §04. They include the full bonus structure (~25% of GGR — welcome, reload, cashback, free spins, VIP combined), Cubeia platform share (5%), blended game-provider share (~10.5%), crypto payment processing (~3%), and ~$16.6K monthly fixed opex after the Owl base correction. The figures are approximations — actual results move with product mix, bonus uptake, and provider terms — and are intended to make the scale of the business visible, not to serve as exact forecasts.
Investor Has the bonus structure been factored into these figures, or is there a hidden cost waiting to surface?
Yes, fully. The 25% bonus figure is the blended impact of every player-facing promotion combined — welcome, reload, cashback, free spins, and VIP rewards. It is intentionally on the higher side of typical crypto-casino ratios, so projections do not understate cost. If actual bonus uptake runs lower, Net Profit will come in higher than these figures suggest. There is no separate bonus line waiting elsewhere in the cascade.
