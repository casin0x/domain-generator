03 Brazil Market Benchmarks — Everything Else

Brazil regulated its market in Jan 2025 (Lei 14.790) and posted ~$7B GGR in year one. It is a high-volume, low-ARPU, PIX-driven, bonus-sensitive market — the opposite profile to Europe. These are the figures the model should use for a Brazil-first launch.

The Brazil shape: low CPA and low ARPU. Acquisition is cheap, but each player is worth little per month — so the model lives or dies on retention and whale concentration, not on acquisition cost. That is why the retention % above is the single most leveraged input.
04 Unit Economics — Brazil ARPU × Retention %

Combining the two grounded inputs — Brazil ARPU and the operator retention curve — gives an illustrative GGR-per-acquired-player and CPA payback. Expected active-months = 1 / (1 − monthly retention); lifetime GGR = ARPU × active-months; net contribution ≈ 45% of GGR (after ~30% rev-share, ~12% provider/platform, ~10% bonus).

Paid CPA acquisition at blended ARPU does not pay back within 6 months at any realistic retention level. That is normal for Brazil — operators make money via (1) whale concentration (ARPPU R$250–400 on the top ~6%), (2) rev-share affiliates instead of fixed CPA (pay only on real revenue), and (3) low-cost organic/community channels. The model should lean on these three, not on paid CPA volume.
05 What to Put Into the Forecast
  • ARPU: model a blended R$160 (~$30)/active player·mo, plus a separate whale/VIP cohort at R$250–400 ARPPU. Don’t use a single flat number — Brazil revenue is whale-concentrated.
  • CPA: R$200 (~$38) base for paid/affiliate; assume a large share of volume comes from rev-share (25–40%) and organic rather than fixed CPA.
  • Retention: 19% monthly base / 30% engineered target; week-4 cohort 8% → 15% target. This is the lever the retention hire owns.
  • Payments: build PIX-first (96% of the market); support R$5 micro-deposits; expect frequent small top-ups, not large single deposits.
  • Bonus: 100%-to-R$100–500 welcome is the norm, but gate it hard with the T&C arsenal (1× wagering, withdrawal velocity caps) — Brazil is bonus-sensitive and abuse-prone.
  • Don’t import EU economics. Brazil is low-CPA / low-ARPU / high-volume. A model tuned to European ARPU will overstate revenue badly.
06 Sources & Caveats
  • Retention % source: the retention lead’s prior crypto casino (anonymised), Sep 2025–Mar 2026. Only the retention percentages are used — no counts, deposits, or revenue.
  • Brazil benchmarks are market estimates (2025–2026) from Statista, Scaleo, GameOn, iGamingToday and SBC reporting — treat as directional ranges, not GoLuna actuals. Validate against GoLuna’s own first cohorts once live.
  • ARPU is definition-sensitive: per-registered-user ARPU is lower; per-paying-user (ARPPU) is higher. Brazil revenue is heavily whale-skewed, so a single average is misleading — model a head and a tail.
  • Unit-economics table is illustrative, built from Brazil ARPU × the observed retention curve with assumed margin splits. It shows the shape (paid CPA is hard; retention/whales/rev-share carry it), not a precise GoLuna P&L.
  • FX at R$5.4 ≈ $1; BRL/USD moves, so dollar figures are approximate.
