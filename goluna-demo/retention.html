Retention Benchmark — Real Operator Data

Actual cohort and churn data from the retention lead’s prior crypto casino (a comparable whitelabel book) — 630 first-time depositors over 7 months. Used here to pressure-test the retention and LTV assumptions baked into the GoLuna forecast. The short version: the assumptions are optimistic against this benchmark, and the gap is the entire reason the retention hire matters.

Headline finding.  On this benchmark, blended 6-month GGR per FTD landed around $10–30 — the GoLuna forecast and revenue P&L assume ~$130. The driver: only ~8% of depositors are still active 30 days in, and freshly-acquired players were net GGR-negative in aggregate (they extract the welcome bonus and leave). The money lives entirely in the small retained tail. This is the bonus-hunter dynamic from Paths A/B — now with hard numbers behind it.

04 GGR by Player Segment — The Real Story

Average GGR per player per month, weighted by segment population. This is the single most important table on the page: newly-acquired players were net GGR-negative, retained players generated ~$118/mo, and reactivated (won-back) players ~$55/mo. The implication is blunt — acquisition volume alone destroys value; the book only works if players are retained and reactivated.

SegmentAvg GGR / player·monthWhat it means
Retained (carried over)+$118The profit core. Worth ~5× a new player before bonus cost.
Reactivated (won back)+$55High ROI — no fresh CPA, just a reactivation touch.
New (first month)–$24Net-negative in aggregate after welcome bonus. You pay to acquire and lose money month 0.
The –$24 new-player figure is exactly what the T&C arsenal is designed to fix: wagering requirements, withdrawal velocity caps, and bonus-forfeiture clauses turn the month-0 extraction into a smaller (or positive) number. Retention and bonus-gating are the same problem viewed from two sides.

06 What It Means for GoLuna
  • Re-anchor the LTV assumption. Carry three cases in the forecast: bear $20–30, base $50–80, bull $130. The current single-point $130 overstates M1–M6 cumulative NP — by the ~$80–120K already flagged on the Paths page.
  • Instrument D30 from day one. The one number that decides everything is week-4 cohort retention. Benchmark 7.7%; target ≥15%. If GoLuna’s first two cohorts sit near 8%, the model is the bear case and spend should slow.
  • Spend on reactivation, not just acquisition. Won-back players are +$55/mo at near-zero CPA. A reactivation flow (the Path-B 7-day dormant trigger) is the highest-ROI lever in the data.
  • Gate the welcome bonus harder. The –$24 month-0 figure is a bonus-design problem. The T&C arsenal (1× deposit wagering, withdrawal caps, forfeiture) directly attacks it.
  • This validates Path B. The benchmark is the evidence the bonus-hunter thesis was right. Path B’s retention-first reshuffle is the correct structural response, not a stylistic preference.
  • The retention hire is the bet. Their mandate, in one line: move D30 from ~8% toward 15%+ and turn month-0 new GGR from –$24 toward break-even. That delta is the difference between the bear and base cases.
07 Caveats — Read Before Quoting These Numbers
  • Small, single-operator sample. 630 FTDs, 7 months, one casino. Directionally strong, statistically thin in the late-2025 / early-2026 weekly cohorts (some <10 players).
  • This is the “before” book. If these are the numbers the retention lead inherited or improved on, GoLuna’s engineered curve could beat them. If they’re the lead’s best achieved result, treat them as the realistic ceiling. Worth clarifying with them directly.
  • GGR ≠ NGR ≠ profit. These are gross-gaming-revenue figures before affiliate, provider, and bonus-liability deductions. Net contribution per FTD is lower still.
  • Currency is mixed. Source reported in crypto/fiat units; treated as ≈USD. Don’t over-index on the exact dollar figures — the curve shape and segment ratios are the signal.
  • Different brand, market, and bonus structure. GoLuna’s offer, geos, and T&C stack differ. The benchmark transfers as a prior, not a forecast.
