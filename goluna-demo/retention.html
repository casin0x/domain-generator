03 Brazil Market Benchmarks — Everything Else

Brazil regulated its market in Jan 2025 (Lei 14.790) and posted ~$7B GGR in year one. It is a high-volume, low-ARPU, bonus-sensitive market. Onshore betting is PIX-only and crypto betting is outlawed onshore — so GoLuna runs as an offshore crypto-only casino (short term), addressing the crypto-comfortable slice of Brazilian players (~30% crypto adoption), not the full PIX-using base. The figures below are the market backdrop; crypto-only adjustments are flagged per row.

Crypto-only is a TAM filter, not just a payment choice.  ~96% of regulated Brazilian betting runs on PIX. By taking crypto only, GoLuna self-selects to the crypto-literate minority — fewer players, but typically higher-value and more sophisticated (which also means more arbitrage/bonus-savvy, so the T&C gating matters more). PIX’s role here is purely a fiat→crypto on-ramp bridge, an extra step in the funnel. Adding native PIX/fiat later would unlock the mass market — that is the single biggest TAM lever once licensing allows it.

Acquisition is still relatively cheap, but volume is capped by the crypto-only filter — so the model lives or dies on retention and whale concentration, not on raw acquisition volume. That is why the retention % above is the single most leveraged input, and why a future fiat/PIX on-ramp is the biggest growth unlock.
04 Unit Economics — Brazil ARPU × Retention %

Combining the two grounded inputs — crypto-only ARPU (~$56/mo) and the operator retention curve — gives an illustrative GGR-per-acquired-player and CPA payback. Expected active-months = 1 / (1 − monthly retention); lifetime GGR = ARPU × active-months; net contribution ≈ 45% of GGR (after ~30% rev-share, ~12% provider/platform, ~10% bonus). Even at crypto-only ARPU, paid CPA only approaches break-even at high retention within 6 months — it never fully pays back at the 19% base. The crypto filter helps (higher ARPU than the mass market) but doesn’t rescue paid volume on its own. Operators make the money via (1) whale concentration (ARPPU $46–74 on the top players), (2) rev-share affiliates instead of fixed CPA, and (3) low-cost organic/community channels. Lean on these three plus retention, not on paid CPA volume.
05 What to Put Into the Forecast
  • ARPU: for a crypto-only book, model nearer the ARPPU band $46–74 rather than the market-wide blended ~$30 — the crypto filter removes the micro-bettor tail. Still split a whale/VIP cohort out; revenue is whale-concentrated.
  • CPA: ~$46 for paid/affiliate (upper end — crypto pool is narrower); assume a large share of volume comes from rev-share (25–40%) and organic rather than fixed CPA.
  • Retention: 19% monthly base / 30% engineered target; week-4 cohort 8% → 15% target. This is the lever the retention hire owns.
  • Payments: crypto only short term — USDT-on-Tron default, plus BTC/ETH/LTC. No fiat. Treat PIX as a fiat→crypto on-ramp the player handles off-platform; budget for the funnel drop it causes. Model native PIX/fiat as a later TAM-expansion phase, not the launch.
  • Bonus: 100%-to-~$50–200 crypto welcome is the norm, but gate it hard with the T&C arsenal (1× wagering, withdrawal velocity caps) — the crypto audience is bonus-sensitive and abuse-savvy.
  • Don’t import EU economics. Brazil is low-CPA / lower-ARPU than Europe; a model tuned to European ARPU will overstate revenue. But crypto-only lifts per-player value above the broad Brazil average — model the crypto segment, not the mass market.
06 Sources & Caveats
  • Retention % source: the retention lead’s prior crypto casino (anonymised), Sep 2025–Mar 2026. Only the retention percentages are used — no counts, deposits, or revenue.
  • Brazil benchmarks are market estimates (2025–2026) from Statista, Scaleo, GameOn, iGamingToday and SBC reporting — treat as directional ranges, not GoLuna actuals. Validate against GoLuna’s own first cohorts once live.
  • ARPU is definition-sensitive: per-registered-user ARPU is lower; per-paying-user (ARPPU) is higher. Brazil revenue is heavily whale-skewed, so a single average is misleading — model a head and a tail.
  • Unit-economics table is illustrative, built from Brazil ARPU × the observed retention curve with assumed margin splits. It shows the shape (paid CPA is hard; retention/whales/rev-share carry it), not a precise GoLuna P&L.
  • FX at R$5.4 ≈ $1; BRL/USD moves, so dollar figures are approximate.
