Cambria — Due Diligence Report

Full competitive analysis of the closest rival to Hotshot in the skill-based crypto PvP wagering space. For team, investors, and marketing.

Primary Competitor — Tempest Labs
01 Executive Summary
Cambria proves the market for crypto PvP wagering exists ($170M+ claimed volume, 128K players). But the game is shallow (2D stance-switching), niche (RuneScape nostalgia), has no casino integration, no mobile, no gambling license, and a pseudonymous team. A skill-based PvP shooter with casino integration would be genuinely differentiated.

04 Product Breakdown
Core PvP

Duel Arena

1v1 PvP where both players stake ETH or RON. Winner takes all, settled on-chain.

  • Stance-switching combat (aggressive / accurate / defensive)
  • Ranked ladder system with ELO
  • Excalibur progressive jackpot (2.5% of volume)
  • 4% trigger chance on each duel
  • On-chain escrow for wagers
Seasonal

Gold Rush

10-14 day seasonal MMO mode. All players reset to level 1 at start.

  • $35 Royal Charter = season pass entry
  • Prize pool funded from player spending
  • Grind, PvP, trade, guild warfare
  • Seasonal resets create urgency
  • Top performers earn from prize pool

08 Red Flags
9 material concerns identified. Several represent existential regulatory and trust risks that Hotshot must avoid by design.
01 No gambling license, no KYC, no geo-blocking — at $170M+ claimed volume. Operating in a regulatory gray zone.
02 Pseudonymous team — no publicly identified founder or leadership. No verifiable LinkedIn, company registry, or legal entity.
03 No smart contract audit publicly referenced. Users trust funds to unaudited on-chain escrow.
04 Centralized server determines winners — chain only escrows funds. Outcome integrity relies entirely on trust.
05 Wagering contract addresses not published. The $170M+ volume claim is entirely unverifiable on-chain.
06 Token allocation opaque — no team/investor/treasury split disclosed. Presale buyers have zero visibility on dilution.
07 NFTs in heavy decline — Founders floor down 67% YoY, Companions down 66%. Market confidence eroding.
08 Player loss report: $5,168 spent + 200 hours invested = only $893 returned. Deeply negative player economics.
09 Pure luck mini-games (Flower Poker, Dicing) undermine the entire "skill game" legal defense. Any regulator would classify these as gambling.
09 Hotshot vs Cambria

Where Hotshot Wins

  • Real skill depth: FPS aim and movement vs shallow stance-switching
  • Genre: Shooters are the #1 gaming genre globally vs RuneScape niche
  • Mobile potential: Proven by PUBG Mobile, Free Fire at scale
  • Casino integration: Direct funnel into GoLuna ecosystem
  • Streamability: Shooter gameplay is inherently more watchable
  • Persistent progression: No forced seasonal resets
  • Regulation: Can build compliance from day one (Malta Type 4)

What to Steal

  • 4% rake model — clean, transparent, scales with volume
  • Excalibur progressive jackpot — brilliant retention mechanic
  • Content creator investors = built-in distribution channel
  • Multi-chain support from day one
  • Spectator mode drives social engagement and virality
  • Seasonal tournaments create urgency and prize pool FOMO
  • On-chain settlement builds crypto-native trust
