01The Model — What Anzil Does

Anzil is a single contracting + reporting partner for the whole acquisition engine. Phase 1 is acquisition-only; retention is GoLuna’s (or a later phase).

  • Markets. Brazil (primary) + Chile / Argentina test. Scale all three in Phase 2.
  • Channels. Meta media (buying, segmentation, retargeting) · WhatsApp acquisition (Brazil’s highest-engagement channel) · influencer activation · creative & PT-BR/ES localization · weekly CAC-by-market reporting.
  • Payments enablement. Anzil bridges fiat↔crypto incl. PIX into GoLuna’s crypto wallet — the on-ramp that lets Brazilian players fund a crypto-only casino.
  • GoLuna-led allocation. GoLuna prepays all media and owns the player + LTV data, so GoLuna decides where budget scales based on CAC↔LTV.
  • Phase 2 (later). Full retention/CRM, SMS broadcast (hybrid CPA + rev-share), performance (FTD/NGR) alignment, and content-clipping sourcing.
This solves the PIX problem. GoLuna is crypto-only, but ~96% of Brazilian betting runs on PIX. Anzil’s payments enablement is the fiat→crypto on-ramp — players pay PIX, GoLuna settles crypto. (Transaction fee on processed volume, confirmed at integration.)

No optimism baked in.

What still has to hold

  • Real CAC ≈ Anzil’s. If crypto/PIX friction pushes CAC to ~$40, the low-retention case drops under 1× LTV:CAC — dead. The M2 test settles it.
  • ARPU reaches ~market. $30 is the regulated average; a new crypto brand may start below it. Track real GGR/player from week 1.
  • PIX fee lands ≤ the 5% high guess. Every point over 5% comes straight off the 51.5% margin and lengthens payback.
  • Retention clears at least the low window. Below ~2-month average life, even cheap CAC won’t compound — spend the leftover on the retention engine to push toward medium.

★How Realistic Is Each Outcome — The Honest Read

All four scenarios above are possible — they are not equally likely. For a brand-new, unlicensed, crypto-only book acquiring bonus-led players in hyper-competitive Brazil, here’s where the odds actually sit.

Honest base case: unassisted, this lands at Low — a kill or marginal read at M4. The model only clears if you engineer retention up to Medium+ before the bridge runs out. So the pilot’s real job isn’t “acquire players” — it’s “prove we can keep them.”

What moves it up

  • Fund the retention engine (the ~$55K leftover): reload / win-back flows via Customer.io + VIP fast-track — the single lever that drags Low → Medium.
  • Filter bonus hunters — tighter wagering / smaller hooks so the retained cohort is real players, not offer-chasers.
  • Lift ARPU above $30 — a genuine crypto / VIP-heavy book needs fewer actives to clear break-even.
  • Bulletproof PIX↔crypto withdrawals — a failed cash-out is the #1 churn cause in Brazil.

Calibration, not a forecast — industry D30 retention (~15–25% avg, 30–40% best-in-class) applied to a cold-launch, unlicensed, crypto-only Brazil book. Verify against your real M2 cohort at M4.
