Path C — Anzil Media Collaboration

Instead of running acquisition in-house (Paths A & B), GoLuna hands LATAM player acquisition to Anzil Media — one accountable partner running Meta media, WhatsApp, influencers and fiat↔crypto payments across Brazil, Chile & Argentina. This page maps exactly what Anzil charges, how a 2M SEK budget flows through it, and whether the marketing allocation is actually enough.

01The Model — What Anzil Does

Anzil is a single contracting + reporting partner for the whole acquisition engine. Phase 1 is acquisition-only; retention is GoLuna’s (or a later phase).

  • Markets. Brazil (primary) + Chile / Argentina test. Scale all three in Phase 2.
  • Channels. Meta media (buying, segmentation, retargeting) · WhatsApp acquisition (Brazil’s highest-engagement channel) · influencer activation · creative & PT-BR/ES localization · weekly CAC-by-market reporting.
  • Payments enablement. Anzil bridges fiat↔crypto incl. PIX into GoLuna’s crypto wallet — the on-ramp that lets Brazilian players fund a crypto-only casino.
  • GoLuna-led allocation. GoLuna prepays all media and owns the player + LTV data, so GoLuna decides where budget scales based on CAC↔LTV.
  • Phase 2 (later). Full retention/CRM, SMS broadcast (hybrid CPA + rev-share), performance (FTD/NGR) alignment, and content-clipping sourcing.
This solves the PIX problem. GoLuna is crypto-only, but ~96% of Brazilian betting runs on PIX. Anzil’s payments enablement is the fiat→crypto on-ramp — players pay PIX, GoLuna settles crypto. (Transaction fee on processed volume, confirmed at integration.)

04Revenue & Profit — After the Flexible % Fees

Beyond the fixed spend above, money flows out as %-of-something: Anzil’s 15% media management and payments fee, plus the standard variable stack on GGR. The upside: in Phase 1 there’s no 9% affiliate commission (Anzil is a media-fee partner, not rev-share), so net contribution is 56.5% of GGR — 9 points above Paths A/B.

The same Path C economics run forward at five monthly-GGR targets for Brazil. Because Anzil is a media-fee partner (no 9% affiliate), net contribution is 56.5% of GGR. Every cost line is included — player bonuses, game-provider fees, crypto processing, the Cubeia platform fee, fixed opex, and growth marketing (with Anzil’s 15% on top). All figures are USD, per month.

Read the assumptions. The variable rates are fixed by the model and exact. Fixed opex and growth marketing are scenario estimates that scale with size — adjust them for your own plan. This assumes GoLuna’s offshore crypto model (Costa Rica corp) continues, so Brazil’s 12% GGR betting tax and R$30M local licence do not apply — deliberate, but a real regulatory risk. Contribution margin is locked at ~56.5% — every $1 of GGR throws off ~56.5¢ before opex and marketing. Operating leverage then widens net margin from ~27% at $100K to ~33% at $10M as fixed costs dilute. The two biggest levers are bonuses (25%) and marketing (20%) — together 45% of GGR; trimming bonuses 5pts at maturity adds ~5pts straight to the bottom line.
  • Early-stage runs thinner. To grow the book you’d spend 30%+ of GGR on acquisition (lower or negative net) — the 20% above is a steady-state assumption. Mature, you trend to 10–15% and margins widen further.
  • Bonuses are the swing cost. 25% of GGR is acquisition-phase aggressive. A retained, VIP-heavy book typically runs 12–18% — each 1pt is 1pt of net margin.
  • At $5M+/mo, re-examine Anzil’s 15%. A 15% override on a $1M+/mo media budget is $150K+/mo to Anzil. At that scale, bringing acquisition partly in-house likely pays for itself — model both before committing long-term.
  • Fixed opex is an estimate. The lean launch run-rate is ~$9.3K/mo; the higher tiers assume you staff CS, VIP management, compliance and finance as volume grows. Tune to your actual hiring plan.
06Is the Allocation Enough?
It’s a real 3-month pilot — not the full program, and you’ve said you won’t add more. Of the $112.5K growth budget, ~$87.5K goes to Anzil (≈$69K paid media after their fees) and $25K to clipping (M5 organic/content). The paid media = ~30% of Anzil’s €215K media example → ~2,800–5,800 paid FTDs, with clipping adding organic reach on top. Their full 3-market program (€300K ≈ 3.4M SEK) is bigger than your whole budget — so the rest scales from revenue, not more capital.

You’ve fixed the cap, so scaling comes from revenue — not a bigger cheque:

  • Reinvest revenue into media. Run the ~$69K paid pilot, then fund the scale from M2–M4 GGR — how Anzil’s own ramp works ($30K→$75K→$110K).
  • Brazil-only first. Concentrate on the cheapest, highest-PIX market before testing Chile/Argentina (higher CAC).
  • Hold the line. $112.5K is the ceiling — ~$87.5K Anzil + $25K clipping; the ~$8K buffer stays for genuine surprises, not media top-ups.
