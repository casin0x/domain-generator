Path C — Anzil Media Collaboration

Instead of running acquisition in-house (Paths A & B), GoLuna hands LATAM player acquisition to Anzil Media — one accountable partner running Meta media, WhatsApp, influencers and fiat↔crypto payments across Brazil, Chile & Argentina. This page maps exactly what Anzil charges, how a 2M SEK budget flows through it, and whether the marketing allocation is actually enough.

Figures in USD (SEK / EUR where useful), at 1 USD ≈ 10.5 SEK · 1 EUR ≈ $1.08 ≈ 11.34 SEK. Anzil’s own proposal is in EUR and marked a preliminary working draft — CACs and volumes are indicative, not guaranteed.
01The Model — What Anzil Does

Anzil is a single contracting + reporting partner for the whole acquisition engine. Phase 1 is acquisition-only; retention is GoLuna’s (or a later phase).

  • Markets. Brazil (primary) + Chile / Argentina test. Scale all three in Phase 2.
  • Channels. Meta media (buying, segmentation, retargeting) · WhatsApp acquisition (Brazil’s highest-engagement channel) · influencer activation · creative & PT-BR/ES localization · weekly CAC-by-market reporting.
  • Payments enablement. Anzil bridges fiat↔crypto incl. PIX into GoLuna’s crypto wallet — the on-ramp that lets Brazilian players fund a crypto-only casino.
  • GoLuna-led allocation. GoLuna prepays all media and owns the player + LTV data, so GoLuna decides where budget scales based on CAC↔LTV.
  • Phase 2 (later). Full retention/CRM, SMS broadcast (hybrid CPA + rev-share), performance (FTD/NGR) alignment, and content-clipping sourcing.
This solves the PIX problem. GoLuna is crypto-only, but ~96% of Brazilian betting runs on PIX. Anzil’s payments enablement is the fiat→crypto on-ramp — players pay PIX, GoLuna settles crypto. (Transaction fee on processed volume, confirmed at integration.)

04Is the Allocation Enough?
Short answer: enough for a real test pilot, not for Anzil’s full program. The ~$54K media is only ~23% of Anzil’s own €215K media example, and their full €300K program is ~3.4M SEK — bigger than the entire 2M SEK budget. On 2M SEK you fund a genuine test-and-learn, then scale winners from revenue.

If you want to run Anzil at scale on 2M SEK, the levers are:

  • Reinvest revenue into media. Start lean ($54K), let M2–M3 GGR fund the M3+ scale (Anzil’s own ramp is built this way: $30K→$75K→$110K).
  • Brazil-only. Drop the Chile/Argentina test (higher CAC) and concentrate the $54K on the cheapest, highest-PIX market.
  • Raise the budget toward 3M+ SEK if you want the full 3-market program from day one.
  • Trim GoLuna fixed. Defer Betby/Designer ($7.8K) past the pilot to free more media — but the core ($5K staff, security, Claude, Cubeia fees) stays.
05Profit Margins — All Fees Included

One big upside of the Anzil model: in Phase 1 there’s no 9% affiliate commission (Anzil is a media-fee, not a rev-share partner). So net contribution rises to 56.5% of GGR — 9 points above Paths A/B (47.5%).

Acquisition cost (the Anzil media + fees) sits outside this %-stack — it’s the upfront CAC, front-loaded in the 3-month pilot and then it stops. Post-pilot, the retained cohorts pay out against just the GoLuna fixed base:

Post-pilot monthly break-even ≈ $18K GGR/mo ($10K fixed ÷ 56.5%). The real question isn’t the margin — it’s whether the ~$54K pilot media buys enough retained players to reach that. With the operator’s ~19% monthly retention benchmark (see Assumptions), expect the lean pilot to validate, not to fully fund, the scale-up.
