03.5 Bonus Liability, RTP & Bankroll — How They Interact

Three separate cash pools are involved in running a crypto casino, and they get confused easily. This section sets out what each one is, where it comes from, and what eats into what. Calibrated against current GoLuna configuration.

Pool separation — what comes from where

Pool / liability What it covers How it's modelled Amount / rate
Performance bonuses Welcome match, reload, cashback, free spins, VIP rewards — tied to player wagering activity Variable cost line in §06.5 P&L cascade (flows from GGR, not pre-funded) 25% of GGR
Promotional pool new Community giveaways (Telegram/Discord contests), race/tournament prize boosts, streamer-exclusive codes, whale courtesy bonuses — NOT tied to player wagering Explicit budget line on Forecast page (M2–M4 at $4K/mo) $12K total M2–M4
Bankroll Variance buffer for real-money player wins — the cash that pays out when players win big on real wagers Reserved separately from operating budget (500K SEK locked + deferred-payout T&Cs on big wins) 500K SEK / ~$48K
Operating buffer Unforeseen ops costs — what's left at M6 after planned spend M6 cumulative budget remaining (post Promotional pool: ~$7K) ~$7K M6
Bonus payouts and RTP do NOT eat from bankroll directly. Bonus credits are funded by operating cash (modelled at 25% of GGR), and RTP is a mathematical property of games, not a cash draw. What DOES eat from bankroll is variance on real-money wagers — players winning big runs on slots, sportsbook, or poker.

Higher RTP doesn't cost more bankroll directly — it costs more variance. Choosing 97% slots over 96% means lower house edge (less GGR per wager) AND more swings (bigger bankroll need). The bankroll multiplier reflects what's required to absorb a bad month without insolvency risk.

04 Optimal RTP & Margin Configuration
Vertical Target RTP / Hold GGR yield on wagers Why this level
Casino slots RTP 96% 4% of wagers Slightly above industry avg (95%). Matches Stake.com tier · attracts crypto-native players who check published RTPs.
Live casino RTP 98% 2% of wagers European blackjack / roulette standard. Lower margin per hand but very high volume + retention.
Sports (Betby) RTP 93–94% 6–7% margin Sportsbook hold typical 5–8%. Major leagues run leaner (4–5%), niche markets fatter (8–10%). Betby calibrates odds.

Strategic read on RTP: running slots at 96% instead of 95% costs you ~20% of slot GGR but reads as "fair" to crypto-native audiences (who compare RTPs across casinos). That credibility translates into deposit frequency and retention, more than offsetting the per-spin margin loss. The Stake.com playbook.

Leaver clause Default: if a member departs (voluntarily or otherwise), their pool share ends at the departure date. No clawback on previously paid months. Their share is not redistributed to the remaining two — it stays with the company. (Alternative if preferred: 60-day tail then ends.)
Investor compensation Decision-making investors are compensated via equity returns (dividends, exit). They do not draw from this pool. Pool sits as an opex line above the dividend distribution waterfall.
Underperformance / disputes Pool share is tied to membership in the role, not individual KPIs. If performance becomes an issue, the conversation is "role change or departure" — not "reduce your pool share". Keeps the loyalty mechanic clean and non-political.
Re-evaluation trigger Structure is re-evaluated when (a) headcount exceeds 6, (b) any member's role materially expands beyond current scope, or (c) any individual cap binds for 3+ consecutive months ($1.25M+ NP for Owl, $1.67M+ NP for Toro/Gibbiana). Until then, the structure stays as written.
Documentation Once the first profitable month arrives, this becomes a written side-letter between the company and each member. Do not rely on this dashboard alone as the agreement of record.
