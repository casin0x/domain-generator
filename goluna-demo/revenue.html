04 Optimal RTP & Margin Configuration
Vertical Target RTP / Hold GGR yield on wagers Why this level
Casino slots RTP 96% 4% of wagers Slightly above industry avg (95%). Matches Stake.com tier · attracts crypto-native players who check published RTPs.
Live casino RTP 98% 2% of wagers European blackjack / roulette standard. Lower margin per hand but very high volume + retention.
Sports (Betby) RTP 93–94% 6–7% margin Sportsbook hold typical 5–8%. Major leagues run leaner (4–5%), niche markets fatter (8–10%). Betby calibrates odds.
Poker 5% rake (cap $3) 5% of pot Industry standard cash-game rake. Tournament fees 5–10% of buy-in. Lower margin but sticky players.
Strategic read on RTP: running slots at 96% instead of 95% costs you ~20% of slot GGR but reads as "fair" to crypto-native audiences (who compare RTPs across casinos). That credibility translates into deposit frequency and retention, more than offsetting the per-spin margin loss. The Stake.com playbook.

08 Open Questions / Confirm Before Locking
QuestionWhy it matters
Betby exact rev share + monthly minimum Model uses 12% / $3K placeholder. Real number could be 8–15% rev share and $2–5K monthly. Each 1% on Betby rev share = ~$300/mo on $25K sports GGR.
Vertical mix assumption (Casino 65% / Sports 25% / Originals or Poker 10%) Drives blended provider-fee rate. Heavy slots (80%+) would push blended fee higher (~12% blended), reducing net margin.
Welcome bonus economics — is 100% / $500 / 35× the right level? Lower bonus (50% / $250) cuts cost-to-acquire but hurts conversion from clipping/BH Ads creatives. Test in M1–M2.
Casino RTP — 96% or 97%? 97% reads even more "fair" but cuts GGR yield by 25%. Worth testing once organic engagement is measurable.
Crypto chains supported and split BTC has highest network fees; USDT-TRC20 nearly free. Mix changes the 3% crypto-drag number meaningfully.
CAC / LTV calibration after M1 Base case assumes $50 CAC / $100 LTV. Real numbers from first cohort should re-anchor M3–M6 targets up or down.
Sports book hold target with Betby Tighter hold (5%) = more competitive odds = bigger audience. Looser hold (8%) = more GGR per dollar bet. Trade-off depends on traffic.
