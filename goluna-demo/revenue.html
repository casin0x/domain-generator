03.5 Bonus Liability, RTP & Bankroll — How They Interact

Three separate cash pools are involved in running a crypto casino, and they get confused easily. This section sets out what each one is, where it comes from, and what eats into what. Calibrated against current GoLuna configuration.

Pool separation — what comes from where

Pool / liability What it covers How it's modelled Amount / rate
Performance bonuses Welcome match, reload, cashback, free spins, VIP rewards — tied to player wagering activity Variable cost line in §06.5 P&L cascade (flows from GGR, not pre-funded) 25% of GGR
Promotional pool new Community giveaways (Telegram/Discord contests), race/tournament prize boosts, streamer-exclusive codes, whale courtesy bonuses — NOT tied to player wagering Explicit budget line on Forecast page (M2–M4 at $4K/mo) $12K total M2–M4
Bankroll Variance buffer for real-money player wins — the cash that pays out when players win big on real wagers Reserved separately from operating budget (500K SEK locked + deferred-payout T&Cs on big wins) 500K SEK / ~$48K
Operating buffer Unforeseen ops costs — what's left at M6 after planned spend Working budget remaining at M6 on the Path A plan, zero-revenue case (revenue adds to it — see §07 “cash left”) ~$28K M6
Bonus payouts and RTP do NOT eat from bankroll directly. Bonus credits are funded by operating cash (modelled at 25% of GGR), and RTP is a mathematical property of games, not a cash draw. What DOES eat from bankroll is variance on real-money wagers — players winning big runs on slots, sportsbook, or poker.

Higher RTP doesn't cost more bankroll directly — it costs more variance. Choosing 97% slots over 96% means lower house edge (less GGR per wager) AND more swings (bigger bankroll need). The bankroll multiplier reflects what's required to absorb a bad month without insolvency risk.

Bankroll is still the bottleneck even with full T&C protection.  Adopting the full Betplay/Betpanda arsenal cuts the M4 pilot-peak requirement from $244K (at 4×) to $183K (at 3×) — a $61K reduction. But the gap from the current $48K is still ~$135K at the peak, and even the post-pilot GO run-rate needs ~$105K. Combined approach:

• Step 1 — adopt full T&C arsenal at launch. Single biggest lever, zero direct cost. Drop bankroll multiplier from 7.5× to 3×.
• Step 2 — route surplus above the floor into bankroll. At the GO line (~$35K GGR) Net Profit is ~$0, so there is nothing to route — this lever only opens above the floor (at $100K GGR, the 40% bankroll share of the reinvestment split ≈ $12K/mo).
• Step 3 — investor capital-call right. Formalise with trigger: bankroll < 250K SEK → 500K SEK call within 72h. This, not reinvestment, is what actually covers the M2–M4 pilot exposure.
• Step 4 — max-bet caps as last-resort throttle. 0.5–1% of bankroll = $240–480 max bet on launch, scaling with bankroll. Limits worst-case exposure.
04 Optimal RTP & Margin Configuration
Vertical Target RTP / Hold GGR yield on wagers Why this level
Casino slots RTP 96% 4% of wagers Slightly above industry avg (95%). Matches Stake.com tier · attracts crypto-native players who check published RTPs.
Live casino RTP 98% 2% of wagers European blackjack / roulette standard. Lower margin per hand but very high volume + retention.
Sports (Betby) RTP 93–94% 6–7% margin Sportsbook hold typical 5–8%. Major leagues run leaner (4–5%), niche markets fatter (8–10%). Betby calibrates odds.
Poker 5% rake (cap $3) 5% of pot Industry standard cash-game rake. Tournament fees 5–10% of buy-in. Lower margin but sticky players.
Strategic read on RTP: running slots at 96% instead of 95% costs you ~20% of slot GGR but reads as "fair" to crypto-native audiences (who compare RTPs across casinos). That credibility translates into deposit frequency and retention, more than offsetting the per-spin margin loss. The Stake.com playbook.

The three people in this pool — Toro (back-office + anti-fraud), Owl (finance + reporting + ops PM), Gibbiana (affiliate + PR) — are Nordic/German operators in mid-level functional roles. The profit-share below is the mechanism that compensates the early-stage base discount once the business is profitable. It is a loyalty / early-team allocation, not market-rate exec comp. Decision-making investors are compensated separately via equity returns, not this pool.
Envelope trigger ($25K NP): the loyalty envelope activates when monthly Net Profit ≥ $25K. Total envelope is 10% of NP: 5% paid out monthly (2% Owl + 1.5% Toro + 1.5% Gibbiana) and 5% accrues to a Reserve Pool for future loyalty deployment (new hires, scope bumps, discretionary). Each paid share capped at $25K/mo; Reserve accrual paused above $500K balance.

Owl base correction ($50K NP): Owl's base salary moves from $1K → $2K (parity with Toro and Gibbiana) when monthly NP first reaches $50K. Between $25K and $50K NP, Owl receives the same pool share as the others but base stays at $1K. The +$1K bump is company-funded, not from the envelope. Permanent thereafter.

Why this structure

Loyalty comp, not market comp

Compensates ~70% base haircut
Nordic/German mid-level base for these roles ≈ €45–75K/yr. They're on $12–24K/yr base. The pool exists to make them whole-plus-some once the business is actually profitable. At the $25K/mo per-person cap ($300K/yr), total comp lands at ~4–5× their open-market base — appropriate for years of below-market acceptance.

$0 when unprofitable

No cash-flow risk
Pool only triggers above $25K NP. If you never hit profitability, the pool costs nothing. Doesn't conflict with runway / spend-by-M6 strategy.

$25K per-person cap

Predictable upside
Each member capped at $25K/mo individually ($75K total at full cap). Owl's cap binds at $1.25M NP; Toro and Gibbiana at $1.67M NP. Above those, excess profit stays with the company.

5% Reserve for growth

Future hires · bumps · milestones
Half the 10% envelope accumulates as a Reserve Pool — an earmarked future-loyalty obligation, deployable for new hires joining the plan, scope-expansion bumps, or discretionary milestone bonuses. Mutual consent required. Cash stays in operating account until used. Soft cap at $500K.

Differentiated by scope

2% Owl · 1.5% Toro & Gibbiana
Owl's cross-cutting scope (finance + investor reporting + ops PM) earns a 0.5% premium. Toro and Gibbiana on focused functional roles. Small differential keeps the structure non-political while acknowledging the difference.

Monthly settlement

Pay as profits land
Settle each month based on that month's NP. No annual cliff, no clawback. Motivation tracks the trailing month tightly.
Implementation note: the envelope comes out of operating Net Profit before founder distribution. Under the M4-gate model it is dormant for the whole pilot: at the GO line (~$35K GGR) NP ≈ $0, and the $25K trigger needs ~$88K GGR/mo (2.5× the kill line — see §06). Worked example at $100K GGR (NP ≈ $30.8K per §06.5): envelope = 10% × $30.8K = $3.08K. Paid out: Owl $616, Toro and Gibbiana $462 each ($1.54K total); Reserve accrual +$1.54K. The Owl base bump ($50K NP threshold ≈ ~$140K GGR) has not yet triggered there, so Owl base stays at $1K. Owl's paid cap binds at $1.25M monthly NP, Toro and Gibbiana's at $1.67M monthly NP. Reserve accrual continues until the $500K soft cap.

Operating Rules

Rule Detail
Fixed allocation The pool is permanently allocated to Toro, Owl, and Gibbiana. Later hires receive market-rate base + role-appropriate bonuses (or none), not a share of this pool. This is an early-team-only arrangement and does not set precedent for future comp.
Leaver clause Default: if a member departs (voluntarily or otherwise), their pool share ends at the departure date. No clawback on previously paid months. Their share is not redistributed to the remaining two — it stays with the company. (Alternative if preferred: 60-day tail then ends.)
Investor compensation Decision-making investors are compensated via equity returns (dividends, exit). They do not draw from this pool. Pool sits as an opex line above the dividend distribution waterfall.
Underperformance / disputes Pool share is tied to membership in the role, not individual KPIs. If performance becomes an issue, the conversation is "role change or departure" — not "reduce your pool share". Keeps the loyalty mechanic clean and non-political.
Re-evaluation trigger Structure is re-evaluated when (a) headcount exceeds 6, (b) any member's role materially expands beyond current scope, or (c) any individual cap binds for 3+ consecutive months ($1.25M+ NP for Owl, $1.67M+ NP for Toro/Gibbiana). Until then, the structure stays as written.
Documentation Once the first profitable month arrives, this becomes a written side-letter between the company and each member. Do not rely on this dashboard alone as the agreement of record.
