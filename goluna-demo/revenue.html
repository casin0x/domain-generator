04 Optimal RTP & Margin Configuration
Vertical Target RTP / Hold GGR yield on wagers Why this level
Casino slots RTP 96% 4% of wagers Slightly above industry avg (95%). Matches Stake.com tier · attracts crypto-native players who check published RTPs.
Live casino RTP 98% 2% of wagers European blackjack / roulette standard. Lower margin per hand but very high volume + retention.
Sports (Betby) RTP 93–94% 6–7% margin Sportsbook hold typical 5–8%. Major leagues run leaner (4–5%), niche markets fatter (8–10%). Betby calibrates odds.
Poker 5% rake (cap $3) 5% of pot Industry standard cash-game rake. Tournament fees 5–10% of buy-in. Lower margin but sticky players.
Strategic read on RTP: running slots at 96% instead of 95% costs you ~20% of slot GGR but reads as "fair" to crypto-native audiences (who compare RTPs across casinos). That credibility translates into deposit frequency and retention, more than offsetting the per-spin margin loss. The Stake.com playbook.
05 Bonus Structure — Optimal for Crypto Casino

Welcome bonus

First deposit · headline acquisition offer
100% / $500
100% match up to $500, 35× wagering on slots, 7-day expiry. Headline offer for clipping creatives and BH Ads landing pages. Cost-to-acquire offset.

Reload bonus

Weekly / monthly · retention
25% / $100
25% match on weekly deposits up to $100, 30× wagering. Keeps depositors active without inflating bonus cost.

Weekly cashback

Losses-based · soft-loss recovery
10% net losses
10% of weekly net losses returned as bonus (1× wagering). Crypto-native standard. Reduces churn at the worst psychological moment.

Free spins

Daily login / deposit milestones
50–200 FS
Free spins on a featured slot, 30× wagering on winnings. Highly visual for marketing creatives, low actual cost.

VIP cashback ladder

High-roller retention
10–20%
Tiered cashback for top depositors (Silver / Gold / Platinum). Personal manager at Platinum. 20% of base concentrates here.

Rakeback (poker)

Cash-game reward
30% rake
Returns 30% of rake paid each week. Standard for poker rooms · competitive vs PokerStars / GG Network levels.
Bonus cost target: ~25% of GGR. Stake.com runs ~15–20% on the back of strong organic. A new casino with heavy paid acquisition (us) needs to be more generous in early months to convert and retain. After M6 once cohort behaviour stabilises, target ratchets down toward 20%.

09 Early-Team Loyalty Profit-Share
Full plan document. This section is the summary view. The canonical investor-and-team-facing reference — with role descriptions, full projections, two-sided reasoning, calibration tables, operating rules, scope, and Q&A — lives on its own page: Operating Team Profit-Share Plan →
What this section is. The three people in this pool — Toro (back-office + anti-fraud), Owl (finance + reporting + ops PM), Gibbiana (affiliate + PR) — are Nordic/German operators in mid-level functional roles. The profit-share below is the mechanism that compensates the early-stage base discount once the business is profitable. It is a loyalty / early-team allocation, not market-rate exec comp. Decision-making investors are compensated separately via equity returns, not this pool.
Pool trigger ($25K NP): the pool activates when monthly Net Profit ≥ $25K. Pool is 10% of NP, split equally between the 3.

Owl base correction ($50K NP): Owl's base salary moves from $1K → $2K (parity with Toro and Gibbiana) when monthly NP first reaches $50K. Between $25K and $50K NP, Owl receives the same pool share as the others but base stays at $1K. The +$1K bump is company-funded, not from the pool. Permanent thereafter.

Why this structure

Loyalty comp, not market comp

Compensates ~70% base haircut
Nordic/German mid-level base for these roles ≈ €45–75K/yr. They're on $12–24K/yr base. The pool exists to make them whole-plus-some once the business is actually profitable. At the $25K/mo per-person cap ($300K/yr), total comp lands at ~4–5× their open-market base — appropriate for years of below-market acceptance.

$0 when unprofitable

No cash-flow risk
Pool only triggers above $25K NP. If you never hit profitability, the pool costs nothing. Doesn't conflict with runway / spend-by-M6 strategy.

$25K per-person cap

Predictable upside
Each member capped at $25K/mo from the pool ($75K total pool). Cap binds at $750K NP. Above that, excess profit stays with the company for reinvestment / shareholders.

Aligned with shareholders

% of NP, not GGR
Team paid when the business actually makes money. Doesn't reward gross-revenue growth that loses money on bonuses + provider fees.

Equal third split

No politics at 3-person scale
Weighting by individual contribution becomes contentious at this scale. Equal split keeps things simple. Re-evaluate at headcount 6+.

Monthly settlement

Pay as profits land
Settle each month based on that month's NP. No annual cliff, no clawback. Motivation tracks the trailing month tightly.
Implementation note: the pool comes out of operating Net Profit before founder distribution. At $55K M7+ base-case NP (per §06.5), 10% × $55K = $5.5K pool = ~$1.8K/person uplift. Per-person cap binds at $750K monthly NP — above that, every $1 of extra NP stays with the company. Pool stays at 0% until $25K NP — so M1–M6 has no pool impact even if some months are net-positive on contribution.

Operating Rules

Rule Detail
Fixed allocation The pool is permanently allocated to Toro, Owl, and Gibbiana. Later hires receive market-rate base + role-appropriate bonuses (or none), not a share of this pool. This is an early-team-only arrangement and does not set precedent for future comp.
Leaver clause Default: if a member departs (voluntarily or otherwise), their pool share ends at the departure date. No clawback on previously paid months. Their share is not redistributed to the remaining two — it stays with the company. (Alternative if preferred: 60-day tail then ends.)
Investor compensation Decision-making investors are compensated via equity returns (dividends, exit). They do not draw from this pool. Pool sits as an opex line above the dividend distribution waterfall.
Underperformance / disputes Pool share is tied to membership in the role, not individual KPIs. If performance becomes an issue, the conversation is "role change or departure" — not "reduce your pool share". Keeps the loyalty mechanic clean and non-political.
Re-evaluation trigger Structure is re-evaluated when (a) headcount exceeds 6, (b) any member's role materially expands beyond current scope, or (c) the cap binds for 3+ consecutive months at $750K+ NP. Until then, the structure stays as written.
Documentation Once the first profitable month arrives, this becomes a written side-letter between the company and each member. Do not rely on this dashboard alone as the agreement of record.
